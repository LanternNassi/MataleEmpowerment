EMPOWERING RURAL COMMUNITIES

MCEF : Matale community empowerment foundation



BRIDGING THE SOCIAL AND ECONOMIC DIVIDE AMONGST THE VULNERABLE COMMUNITIES OF KYOTERA AND RAKAI.

Matale Community Empowerment Foundation (MCEF) is a Community based foundation which was established in 2023 to empower rural communities through dynamic, diverse but simple programs that directly involve and benefit rural community members, especially children, women, Persons with Disability and the youth regardless of their religion, tribe, sex or physical appearance. The programs are designed to foster mindset change on harmful norms and practices that perpetuate gender-based discrimination and the need to protect vulnerable communities, the relevance of education, to create awareness on the benefits of creating diverse sources of income, equip the beneficiaries with practical skills in various economic activities, and strengthen community and grassroot structures on demanding for accountability from duty bearers through various advocacy initiatives.

VISION

Fostering knowledge and skills that break the chain of poverty and illiteracy within rural communities.

MISSION

To advance gender equality through transformative livelihood support of vulnerable women, children, youth, older persons, PWDs and Persons Living With HIV/AIDs

OBJECTIVES

    To promote education services to the children in rural communities.
    Improved protection and access to social services for vulnerable women,children and men.
    Build a robust advocacy movement to champion policy reform.
    Facilitate youth development through social activities that are sports related.
    Engage the youth and woman in improved subsistence and commercial agriculture.
    Sensitize the communities on how to conserve the environment.

CORE VALUES

    Integrity
    Innovation
    Respect.
    Team work
    Tansparency and accountability

Overview

MCEF began its operations by assisting local children to go to school, with the long-term aim of increasing the capacity of local families to support their children to attain productive education. Since then, MCEF has continuously and tirelessly worked with the different community members through various projects that include Productive education and agricultural support, preventive community health care, advocacy initiatives and environmental conservation.

Through these initiatives we have had strong support from the local communities which has vastly contributed to sustainability of the initiatives. MCEF is developing national and international network of experienced professionals that serve as support team and a wider network of individuals and groups that significantly mentor children. MCEF’s innovative programs are run by experienced, professional, local and national staff and MCEF is well placed to partner with funding bodies and other service providers.

We intend to break the chain of poverty and in due course empower rural communities to sustain their own wellbeing and development. This is directed to be achieved through the direct involvement of the community leadership that drive the community members to work together to achieve the desired change through the support of MCEF.

In a nutshell therefore, MCEF uses community-based approach and a beneficiary-centred approach that draws from the experiences of the target beneficiaries to customize solutions that are aimed to provide a holistic solution to ending the chain of poverty by creating awareness on the benefits of formal education, improving health, fostering mindset change on harmful gender norms and practices that hinder gender equality, and developing commerce through sustainable income-generating projects.

Frequently Asked Questions


What was the inspiration of MCEF?

The founder was driven by the need to advocate for change in mindset within the community upon realizing that at the centre of the abject poverty in the area were the high levels of illiteracy as there was a low uptake of the formal education, the high rates of child marriages, discrimination in resource allocation on the basis of disability, HIV status and harmful gender practices.
